The Body is a Temple
On Physical Discipline
"Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit within you, which you have from God, and that you are not your own? For you were bought with a price; therefore glorify God in your body."
1 Corinthians 6:19–20
Every rep, every mile, every clean meal is an act of worship. You are not destroying a habit — you are reclaiming a temple. God gave you that 6'4" frame and those genetics for a reason. Steward them.
On Temptation
"No temptation has overtaken you except what is common to mankind. And God is faithful; he will not let you be tempted beyond what you can bear. But when you are tempted, he will also provide a way out so that you can endure it."
1 Corinthians 10:13
The craving for nicotine at 2pm is not unique to you. Every man who has ever quit has felt exactly what you feel right now. God has already built the exit ramp. You just have to take it.
On Anxiety & Withdrawal
"Have no anxiety at all, but in everything, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, make your requests known to God. Then the peace of God that surpasses all understanding will guard your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus."
Philippians 4:6–7
Withdrawal is anxiety in the body. When it rises — stop, breathe, pray. Not as weakness. As the most disciplined thing a man can do. The peace that follows is not chemical. It is something else entirely.
On Perseverance
"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me."
Philippians 4:13
Not a bumper sticker. A statement made by a man writing from prison, who had been beaten, shipwrecked, and starved. If Paul could have peace in chains, you can get through day three without a pouch.
On Discipline
"Every athlete exercises discipline in every way. They do it to win a perishable crown, but we an imperishable one. Thus I do not run aimlessly; I do not fight as if I were shadowboxing. No, I drive my body and train it."
1 Corinthians 9:25–27
Paul is describing an athlete's mindset two thousand years before sport psychology existed. You trained for football. You know what discipline costs. This is the same thing — with an eternal prize.
On the Man You're Becoming
"Finally, brothers, whatever is true, whatever is honorable, whatever is just, whatever is pure, whatever is lovely, whatever is gracious — if there is any excellence and if there is anything worthy of praise, think about these things."
Philippians 4:8
This is your mental diet. What you feed your mind shapes what you become. The man she sees — the man your faith calls you to be — is built in moments like this one, when no one is watching.
Augustine — Confessions
✦ Confessions — Book I
"Thou madest us for Thyself, and our heart is restless, until it repose in Thee."
Saint Augustine, Confessions, Book I, Chapter 1
Augustine wrote this after years of chasing pleasure, ambition, and sin — and finding each one hollow. The restlessness you feel when you put down the pouch and don't know what to do with your hands? That is not withdrawal. That is your soul looking for something real. Give it something real.
✦ Confessions — Book VIII
"I was bound, not with another's irons, but by my own iron will. My will the enemy held, and thence had made a chain for me, and bound me. For of a forward will, was a lust made; and a lust served, became custom; and custom not resisted, became necessity."
Saint Augustine, Confessions, Book VIII, Chapter 5
Augustine describing his own addiction — to lust, to habit, to the comfortable chains we choose. He is describing nicotine. He is describing weed. He wrote this sixteen centuries ago. The chains are the same. So is the way out: not willpower alone, but surrendering the will to something greater.
✦ Confessions — Book X
"Our heart is restless until it rests in You. Late have I loved you, O Beauty ever ancient, ever new, late have I loved you! You were within me, but I was outside, and it was there that I searched for you."
Saint Augustine, Confessions, Book X, Chapter 27
He searched everywhere — pleasure, intellect, status — before finding what was already within him. You are 22. Augustine was 32 when he converted. You are not late. You are early. This is the best possible time to build your foundation.
Faith & Stoic Discipline
Marcus Aurelius · Meditations
"You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."
— Marcus Aurelius, Emperor of Rome
A pagan emperor who never knew Christ, yet lived a life of radical self-discipline and duty. The Stoics and the Church Fathers read each other. Thomas Aquinas built his moral theology in conversation with Aristotle. You can honor Marcus and honor God — they both call you to master yourself before you can serve others. The craving does not control you. You do.
Thomas Aquinas · On Virtue
"The things that we love tell us what we are."
— Saint Thomas Aquinas
What do you love? Football. Faith. Her. The man you used to be in that mirror. Let those things tell you who you are — not a pouch, not a habit, not comfort. Aquinas taught that virtue is not the absence of desire but the ordering of desire toward what is truly good. Order your desires. Train them like you trained on the field.
On Leading Her Home
"The soul that loves God has no need for anything else."
— Saint John of the Cross
She is Lutheran. She loves Christ. That is the foundation. What she needs to see is not an argument — it is a man. A man whose faith is visible in how he treats his body, keeps his word, disciplines his habits, and loves with patience. Monica prayed for Augustine for thirty years. The witness of a life is louder than any theology debate. Be the witness. Let the rest belong to God.
On This Exact Moment
"Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good."
— Romans 12:21
The craving is real. The discomfort is real. And your response to it — right now, in this moment — is the formation of your character. Every time you choose the run over the pouch, the prayer over the panic, the discipline over the comfort, you are becoming the man God designed at 6'4" with a heart that can carry more than weight plates.
